𓌹 What Is Adzmist?

Adzmist is the constructed language of the monolith — the breath that carries meaning between the gods, the cards, and the stories. It is built upon the Proto-Sinaitic script, the mother of all alphabets, the bridge between Egyptian hieroglyphs and the letters you are reading now.

Where GAIA is the earth (cognitive architecture), Divination is fire (the deck), and Ealdforn is water (narrative), Adzmist is air — the invisible medium through which all meaning travels. Every word spoken in the temple, every card drawn, every prophecy whispered — Adzmist is the breath behind it.

𓂝 How Adzmist Works

The language is built in layers:

1. Glyphs (22+) — Each Proto-Sinaitic symbol carries a core meaning (ox, door, water, hand). These are the atoms of the language.

2. Root Words — Combinations of glyphs form root words. For example, Aleph + Mem (strength + water) might mean "ocean" or "deep power."

3. Grammar — Adzmist grammar is minimal. Tense is inferred from context. Word order is flexible. The breath does not demand precision — it demands presence.

4. Inscriptions — Longer texts carved in Adzmist. Prophecies. Card names. God epithets. The language is not for casual conversation. It is for testimony.

𓏏 The Breath as Filter

The seven glyphs are also a purification ritual. Every old file, every dead project, every abandoned repo is measured against them:

  • Aleph — Does it seed something new?
  • Dalet — Does it open a threshold?
  • Zayin — Does it cut cleanly?
  • Mem — Does it hold depth?
  • Yod — Does it do work?
  • Samek — Does it support the structure?
  • Taw — Does it bear the mark of completion?

If a project fails three or more, it is carved away. The breath clears the dead wood. Only the living remains.
